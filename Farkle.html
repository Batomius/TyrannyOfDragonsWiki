Farkle

Summary

Farkle is a popular game played with dice across Faerun.


Rules

There are many different rules for Farkle, and they change frequently by region. The most standard rules are discussed below:


A game of Farkle is commonly played by rolling 6 dice. Every roll, a player must select a minimum of 1 scoring dice to keep. They may then choose to re-roll their remaining dice, or to take their scorings for that round and ad it to their game score. If a player is unable to take a dice because there is no possible scoring combination, they are bust, and lose all the points they have accrued during that round (but not those from previous rounds).

If a player manages to clear all six of their dice by collecting scoring dice, they are given the opportunity to roll six more dice and continue their turn. This continues until they either decide to end their turn and take the points they have manged to collect, or until they are unable to continue and are declared bust.

A standard game of Farkle is played to 10,000 points, although shorter games, with a lower target, or longer games with a higher one, are also common.

Regional Variants

In Elturel, Farkle is played with the standard rules, with the additional rule that a a pair of 1s is worth 500 points. This is called 'Twin Suns" and is named in honour of the city's Second Sun.
